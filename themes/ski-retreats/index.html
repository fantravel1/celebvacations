Celebrity Ski Culture

Skiing has been a celebrity pastime since Hollywood's golden age. The combination of athletic challenge, stunning alpine scenery, and exclusive social scenes makes ski destinations perennial A-list favorites.

Aspen pioneered American celebrity ski culture, with stars like Gary Cooper and John Wayne establishing it as the place to be seen in the 1950s. Today, everyone from Mariah Carey (famous for her annual Christmas visits) to the Kardashian clan regularly hits the slopes. The appeal extends beyond skiing itself—luxury chalets, world-class dining, and elite social scenes draw celebrities even if they never touch a ski.

Why Celebrities Love Ski Resorts

  • Natural privacy - goggles and ski gear provide anonymity on the slopes
  • Exclusive accommodations - private chalets and five-star hotels
  • Social scene - concentrated celebrity community during peak weeks
  • Family-friendly - activities for all ages and skill levels
  • Apres-ski culture - legendary parties and nightlife
  • Winter escape - beautiful contrast to California/beach life

American & Canadian Celebrity Ski Destinations

#1 Ski Destination

Aspen, Colorado

America's most glamorous ski destination. Four mountains, legendary apres-ski at Ajax Tavern, Matsuhisa dining, and the Caribou Club. Home to Mariah Carey's Christmas tradition.

Deer Valley, Utah

Ultra-exclusive, skiers-only resort. No snowboards allowed. Immaculate grooming, limited lift tickets, and proximity to Park City/Sundance Film Festival.

78 celebrity visitsPeak: Jan-Feb

Jackson Hole, Wyoming

More rugged, less scene. Harrison Ford, Sandra Bullock own ranches. World-class terrain, authentic Western culture, Amangani resort for ultimate luxury.

67 celebrity visitsPeak: Dec-Mar

Whistler, Canada

North America's largest ski area. Justin Bieber, Ryan Reynolds Canadian connection. Four Seasons and Fairmont properties, Olympic legacy.

54 celebrity visitsPeak: Dec-Apr

Ski Retreats FAQs

Top celebrity ski destinations: Aspen, Colorado (#1 overall), St. Moritz and Gstaad (Switzerland), Courchevel (France), Whistler (Canada), Deer Valley and Park City (Utah), Jackson Hole (Wyoming), Verbier (Switzerland), and Megeve (France). Aspen dominates American celebrity skiing, while St. Moritz and Courchevel are European favorites.

In Aspen, celebrities stay at The Little Nell (only ski-in/ski-out five-star, legendary service), St. Regis Aspen Resort (refined luxury, butler service), or rent private chalets in exclusive neighborhoods like Red Mountain, Starwood, or the West End. Many A-listers own multi-million dollar homes. The Caribou Club is the exclusive nightlife spot.

Peak celebrity ski season runs late December through early March. Christmas week and New Year's are most star-studded in Aspen and St. Moritz. The X Games in Aspen (late January) brings action sports celebrities. European resorts like Courchevel peak during Russian New Year and February school holidays. Spring skiing (March-April) sees celebrities seeking fewer crowds.
