Pebble Beach Golf Links

California's iconic coastal course where celebrities and pros compete

Playing Pebble Beach

Unlike most famous courses, Pebble Beach is open to the public—if you can afford the $595 green fee. The dramatic cliffside holes along Carmel Bay make it bucket-list golf.

For the best experience, stay at The Lodge at Pebble Beach for guaranteed tee times and wake up to views of the 18th fairway. Package rates include golf and luxury accommodations.

The AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am every February pairs PGA Tour pros with celebrities. Bill Murray, Larry the Cable Guy, Alfonso Ribeiro, and numerous athletes participate. Spectators can watch at multiple courses.

Monterey Peninsula Courses

  • Pebble Beach Golf Links: The iconic public course, 7 US Opens
  • Spyglass Hill: Challenging Robert Trent Jones design through forest and dunes
  • Spanish Bay: Links-style course with bagpiper at sunset
  • Cypress Point: Ultra-private, member-only, considered by some the world's best
  • Monterey Peninsula CC: Private club used in AT&T Pro-Am

Celebrity Private Club Favorites

Los Angeles Area

Sherwood Country Club

Thousand Oaks course formerly hosted Tiger Woods' charity event. Members include Wayne Gretzky, Will Smith, and entertainment executives. Known for celebrity-heavy member roster.

The Riviera Country Club

Pacific Palisades classic host of the Genesis Invitational. Celebrity members and LA's most prestigious golf. Larry David is a regular. $500K+ initiation.

Lakeside Golf Club

Burbank's entertainment industry hub. Bing Crosby, Bob Hope history. Current members include Mark Wahlberg and Samuel L. Jackson. Studios nearby make it convenient.

Bel-Air Country Club

Exclusive enclave in the hills. Entertainment and business elite. Jack Nicholson, George Lopez among past members. Legendary privacy.

Florida Celebrity Golf

Hobe Sound private course owned by MJ. Invite-only for friends and business partners. Features his Jumpman logo. Ultimate celebrity course.

Jupiter club designed by Jack Nicklaus. Tiger Woods was a member. Other members include Rickie Fowler, Dustin Johnson, and CEOs.

Seminole Golf Club

Palm Beach ultra-exclusive. Considered one of America's best private courses. Wall Street and old money meets celebrities.

Albany, Bahamas

Tiger Woods-designed course at luxury development. Celebrity homeowners include Justin Timberlake and Joe Lewis.

Global Links

International Celebrity Golf

Scotland: The Home of Golf

St. Andrews Old Course is the world's most famous golf course. Public via ballot system or hotel stays at Old Course Hotel or Rusacks. Celebrities play here for the historical significance.

Gleneagles offers luxury resort golf with three championship courses. The 2014 Ryder Cup venue welcomes celebrities seeking five-star Scottish hospitality.

Royal Dornoch in the Highlands is a pilgrimage for serious golfers. Remote location adds to the mystique. Tom Watson's favorite.

Ireland & England

Ballybunion on Ireland's west coast is a wild links experience. Bill Clinton famously played here and has a statue in town.

Adare Manor combines castle luxury with a top course. Celebrities stay in the restored manor and play the redesigned Tom Fazio course.

Sunningdale near London is a favorite of golf-loving celebrities visiting the UK. Private but prestigious.

Caribbean & Tropical Golf

Sandy Lane, Barbados

Three courses at the famous resort. Tiger Woods married here. Green Monkey course is among the Caribbean's best.

Casa de Campo, Dominican Republic

Teeth of the Dog designed by Pete Dye. Affordable luxury with celebrity-quality course. Popular for group trips.

Kapalua, Maui

Plantation Course hosts PGA Tour's Tournament of Champions. Dramatic Hawaiian views. Celebrity magnet during tournament.

Common Questions

Celebrity Golf FAQs

Which celebrities are avid golfers?

Famous celebrity golfers include Justin Timberlake (5 handicap), Bill Murray (known for AT&T Pro-Am antics), Mark Wahlberg (4 handicap, owns club membership at multiple courses), Samuel L. Jackson (plays frequently in LA), Michael Jordan (owns Bears Club in Jupiter), and Larry David (regular at Riviera). Athletes like Steph Curry, Patrick Mahomes, and Charles Barkley are also passionate golfers.

Where do celebrities play golf in California?

In California, celebrities play at Sherwood Country Club (Tiger Woods former home course), The Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, Bel-Air Country Club, Lakeside Golf Club in Burbank (entertainment industry favorite), and Pebble Beach Golf Links. Private clubs like Sherwood and Bel-Air require membership or guest invitations.

Can you play golf at Pebble Beach like celebrities?

Yes, Pebble Beach Golf Links is a public course, though green fees are around $595. Stay at The Lodge at Pebble Beach or The Inn at Spanish Bay for priority tee times. The AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am in February brings celebrities and pros together. Spyglass Hill and Spanish Bay are also accessible courses in the area.

What is the AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am?

The AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am is an annual PGA Tour event held in February where professional golfers team up with celebrities and amateurs. Famous participants include Bill Murray, Clint Eastwood, Ray Romano, Alfonso Ribeiro, and various athletes. The tournament rotates between Pebble Beach, Spyglass Hill, and Monterey Peninsula Country Club.

Which golf resorts are popular with celebrities?

Top celebrity golf resorts include Pebble Beach Resorts (California), Bandon Dunes (Oregon), Kiawah Island Golf Resort (South Carolina), Reynolds Lake Oconee (Georgia), and Kapalua Resort in Maui. Internationally, celebrities favor St. Andrews in Scotland, Ballybunion in Ireland, and Sandy Lane in Barbados.
