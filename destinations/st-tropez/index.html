About St. Tropez

St. Tropez is the crown jewel of the French Riviera—a sun-drenched Mediterranean paradise where glamour, excess, and exclusivity converge every summer. For celebrities seeking the ultimate European summer experience, no destination compares.

What was once a quiet fishing village transformed into a global celebrity magnet in the 1950s when Brigitte Bardot filmed "And God Created Woman" on its beaches. Since then, St. Tropez has been synonymous with jet-set glamour, attracting everyone from European royalty to Hollywood A-listers and tech billionaires.

The town's legendary appeal lies in its unique combination of elements: a picturesque harbor filled with hundreds of superyachts, world-famous beach clubs along Pampelonne Beach, charming cobblestone streets lined with designer boutiques, and a social scene that operates at a level of luxury few places can match. Summer in St. Tropez is not just a vacation—it's a statement.

Frequently Asked Questions

St. Tropez attracts a wide range of A-list celebrities including Leonardo DiCaprio (regular summer visitor on his yacht), Rihanna, Beyonce and Jay-Z, P. Diddy, Naomi Campbell, and many European royals and fashion icons. It's particularly popular during July and August when the beach club and nightlife scene reaches its peak.

The most famous celebrity beach clubs are Club 55 (the original and most exclusive, requiring advance reservations), Nikki Beach (known for champagne-fueled parties), Bagatelle (trendy French cuisine with entertainment), and Le Club de Cavaliere. Each offers a unique atmosphere from sophisticated to party-focused.

Peak celebrity season runs from late June through August, with the busiest periods around July 14th (Bastille Day) and the first two weeks of August. This is when the harbor fills with superyachts and the beach clubs operate at full capacity. September offers a quieter but still glamorous experience.

Most celebrities arrive by superyacht, anchoring in the famous harbor or at Pampelonne Beach. Others fly private to nearby Nice or Toulon airports, then transfer by helicopter (15-20 minutes) directly to their hotel or yacht. Some drive from Cannes or Monaco along the scenic coastal roads.
