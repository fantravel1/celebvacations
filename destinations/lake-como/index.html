Lake Como

Italy's Most Glamorous Celebrity Retreat

Lombardy, Italy A-List Hotspot
50+ Celebrity Villas
€500+ Avg Hotel/Night
May-Sep Peak Season

Lake Como has been drawing aristocrats and artists for centuries, but it was George Clooney's purchase of Villa Oleandra in 2002 that transformed this Alpine lake into Hollywood's favorite European escape.

The Y-shaped lake, framed by dramatic mountains and dotted with pastel-colored villages, offers the perfect blend of Italian glamour and privacy. Historic villas with private docks, world-class restaurants, and absolute discretion have made Como the premier destination for celebrities seeking la dolce vita.

The Clooney Effect

How one actor transformed an Italian lake into Hollywood's summer capital.

Villa Oleandra

Tier A Verified

George Clooney purchased this 18th-century villa in Laglio for a reported $10 million in 2002. The 25-room estate features a swimming pool, outdoor theater, gym, and private dock. It has become the epicenter of celebrity Lake Como, hosting everyone from the Obamas to Meghan Markle.

Notable Villa Oleandra Guests:

  • Barack & Michelle Obama — Multiple visits, 2019 viral boat photos
  • Meghan Markle & Prince Harry — Pre-wedding visit
  • Cindy Crawford & Rande Gerber — Own neighboring Villa Margherita
  • Matt Damon — Regular summer guest
  • Julia Roberts — Celebrated birthday at the villa

The Como Community

Following Clooney's lead, other celebrities have purchased properties around the lake. Cindy Crawford and Rande Gerber own Villa Margherita next door. Richard Branson has hosted parties at his lakeside estate. The privacy and European sophistication attract those seeking escape from LA scrutiny.

Local Integration

Unlike some celebrity enclaves, Como's famous residents have been embraced by locals. Clooney frequents village restaurants, supports local causes, and has become an unofficial ambassador for the region. This genuine connection adds to the lake's appeal.

Celebrity Villages

Laglio

Clooney's Village

This tiny village of 900 residents is home to Villa Oleandra. The main piazza has become a paparazzi hotspot during summer months. Local restaurants like Il Gatto Nero offer potential celebrity sightings.

Celebrity Residents: George Clooney, Cindy Crawford

Bellagio

The Pearl of Como

The most famous village on the lake, where the three branches meet. Cobblestone streets, luxury boutiques, and the legendary Grand Hotel Villa Serbelloni. A must-visit for any Como trip.

Central Lake Position

Tremezzo

Grand Hotel Territory

Home to the iconic Grand Hotel Tremezzo and Villa Carlotta gardens. The western shore offers stunning sunset views and some of the lake's finest dining.

Western Shore

Cernobbio

Villa d'Este

The southern gateway to Como, home to the legendary Villa d'Este hotel. Annual host to the Concorso d'Eleganza vintage car show that draws global elite.

Southern Lake

Varenna

Eastern Charm

Quieter eastern shore village with Villa Monastero gardens and authentic Italian atmosphere. Less touristy than Bellagio, preferred by those seeking tranquility.

Eastern Shore

Como Town

Gateway City

The main city at the lake's southern tip. Historic cathedral, silk museum, and funicular to Brunate for panoramic views. Easy access from Milan airports.

Southern Tip

Getting There & Around

From Milan

  • Malpensa Airport: 60-90 minutes by car
  • Linate Airport: 60 minutes by car
  • Train: Milan Centrale to Como S. Giovanni (40 min)
  • Helicopter: 15 minutes from Milan heliport

Around the Lake

  • Public Ferry: Connects all villages (slow but scenic)
  • Hydrofoil: Fast service between major towns
  • Private Boat: The celebrity-preferred method
  • Car: Narrow roads, parking challenges in villages

When to Visit

Spring

April - May

Gardens in bloom, mild weather, fewer crowds. Villa Carlotta's azaleas and rhododendrons peak in April.

Summer

June - August

Peak celebrity season. Warm weather, swimming, busiest period. Book months ahead for top hotels.

Fall

September - October

Ideal weather, thinning crowds, harvest season. September sees continued celebrity presence.

Winter

November - March

Many hotels close. Atmospheric and quiet, but limited services. Christmas markets in Como town.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you see George Clooney's villa from a boat?

Yes, Villa Oleandra is visible from the lake and boats regularly pass by. However, it's a private residence, and getting too close is discouraged. The best views are from public ferries or during boat tours that maintain respectful distance.

What is the best hotel on Lake Como for celebrities?

Grand Hotel Tremezzo is the most popular celebrity hotel, known for its iconic floating pool and lakefront position. Villa d'Este in Cernobbio is equally prestigious. Both offer exceptional privacy and five-star service.

How long should I stay at Lake Como?

A minimum of 3 nights is recommended to properly experience the lake. 5-7 nights allows for exploring multiple villages, taking boat trips, and enjoying the relaxed pace. Many celebrities stay for weeks at a time.

Is Lake Como expensive?

Lake Como is one of Europe's most expensive destinations. Luxury hotels start around €500/night in peak season and can exceed €2,000 for top suites. Dining and activities are correspondingly priced. Budget options exist but are limited.
