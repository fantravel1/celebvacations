Mykonos

Greece's Legendary Party Paradise

Cyclades, Greece Global Party Capital
25+ Beach Clubs
€400+ Avg Hotel/Night
Jun-Sep Peak Season

Mykonos is where European glamour meets Greek island beauty. This Cycladic gem has evolved from a bohemian backpacker haven into one of the world's most exclusive summer destinations, drawing celebrities, billionaires, and influencers to its golden beaches and legendary nightlife.

The island's magnetic appeal lies in its perfect contrast: whitewashed villages and ancient windmills by day, world-class beach clubs and VIP nightlife by night. From the champagne-soaked sunbeds of Nammos to the sunset rituals at Scorpios, Mykonos delivers excess and beauty in equal measure.

Legendary Beach Clubs

Where A-listers spend €10,000 days on champagne and sunbeds.

Nammos

Ultimate Status Symbol

The beach club that defined Mykonos luxury. Located on Psarou Beach, Nammos is where oligarchs moor superyachts and celebrities occupy the front row. The restaurant serves exceptional Mediterranean cuisine, but the real product is being seen. Expect to spend €500-1,000 minimum per person, with champagne orders regularly exceeding €50,000.

Nammos Essentials:

  • Reserve sunbeds weeks in advance
  • Front row beds: €800-1,500/day in peak season
  • Dress code: Upscale beach chic
  • Peak hours: 2pm - sunset

Celebrity Sightings: Lindsay Lohan, Leonardo DiCaprio, Naomi Campbell

Mykonos Town (Chora)

Little Venice

The iconic waterfront district where colorful buildings meet the sea. Sunset cocktails at Caprice or Katerina's are essential Mykonos experiences. Expect celebrity sightings as the sun goes down.

The Windmills

Mykonos' signature landmark. The row of 16th-century windmills offers perfect sunset views and photo opportunities. Located steps from Little Venice.

Matogianni Street

The main shopping street lined with luxury boutiques, from Louis Vuitton to local designers. Evening hours see the street transformed into a people-watching paradise.

Old Port

Where fishermen once sold their catch, now superyachts dock and tenders ferry guests ashore. Early morning sees fishing boats; evening brings glamour.

LGBTQ+ Scene

Planning Your Visit

When to Visit

  • Peak Season (Jul-Aug): Maximum energy, highest prices, hardest reservations
  • Shoulder (Jun & Sep): Best balance of weather and crowds
  • Spring (Apr-May): Quiet, affordable, some venues closed
  • Winter: Ghost town—avoid unless seeking solitude

Getting There

  • By Air: Mykonos Airport (JMK), flights from Athens (45 min) and European cities
  • By Sea: Fast ferry from Athens (2.5 hours) or Santorini
  • Private: Charter flights and yacht arrivals common
  • On Island: ATV, car rental, taxis, private drivers

Frequently Asked Questions

How expensive is Mykonos compared to other destinations?

Mykonos is one of Europe's most expensive islands. Budget €300-500/day minimum for comfortable travel. Beach club sunbeds can cost €200-800, cocktails €20-30, and dinner for two €150-300 at upscale restaurants. Luxury travelers should budget €1,000+/day.

Do I need reservations in Mykonos?

Absolutely essential during July and August. Book hotels months ahead, beach club sunbeds at least a week ahead, and popular restaurants 2-3 days minimum. Walking into Nammos without a reservation is essentially impossible in high season.

Is Mykonos good for families?

Mykonos is primarily oriented toward couples and party-goers. Families with children would find Santorini, Crete, or Naxos more suitable. If you do visit with family, stay in quieter areas like Ornos and avoid the party beaches.

What should I wear in Mykonos?

Beach chic is the uniform. Linen, white clothing, designer swimwear, and statement sunglasses dominate. Evening wear trends toward sophisticated but not formal. Pack for heat and looking polished.
