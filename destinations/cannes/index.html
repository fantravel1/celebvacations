About Cannes

Cannes is synonymous with cinematic glamour and Mediterranean luxury. This iconic French Riviera resort city transforms into the global capital of entertainment every May when the Cannes Film Festival draws the world's biggest stars to its legendary red carpet.

Beyond the festival, Cannes maintains its allure year-round. The famous La Croisette promenade stretches along the azure Mediterranean, lined with palatial hotels, designer boutiques, and restaurants where celebrities dine al fresco. The old town of Le Suquet offers charming cobblestone streets with panoramic views, while the harbor hosts some of the world's most impressive superyachts.

The nearby Hotel du Cap-Eden-Roc in Antibes has earned its reputation as the most exclusive celebrity retreat in Europe. During festival season, its legendary pool terrace becomes the ultimate see-and-be-seen destination, hosting amfAR galas and private parties where Hollywood deals are made over champagne and sea views.

Celebrity Travel Occasions

Film Festival

Every May, the world's biggest stars descend for premieres, parties, and the coveted Palme d'Or. The ultimate industry event and social scene.

Yacht Week

The harbor fills with superyachts hosting exclusive parties. Many celebrities prefer staying offshore for maximum privacy and spectacular entertainment.

Summer Escape

June through August offers perfect weather for beach clubs, Mediterranean cruises, and exploring the Riviera's charming villages and vineyards.

amfAR Gala

The annual Cinema Against AIDS gala at Hotel du Cap is the most exclusive charity event in the world, attracting fashion and film royalty alike.

Frequently Asked Questions

Cannes attracts virtually every major Hollywood star, especially during the Film Festival in May. Leonardo DiCaprio is a festival regular known for hosting yacht parties and attending the amfAR gala. Brad Pitt, George Clooney, Tom Cruise, Margot Robbie, and Cate Blanchett are frequent visitors. Directors like Quentin Tarantino and Martin Scorsese present films here, while fashion icons and supermodels arrive for parties and brand events.

During the Cannes Film Festival, hotel prices surge dramatically. Standard rooms at the Carlton or Martinez can exceed $2,000-3,000 per night, while suites range from $5,000-$25,000. Hotel du Cap-Eden-Roc rooms start around $1,500 but premium suites and villas can cost $10,000-$50,000 per night. Many celebrities rent private villas or stay on yachts, which can cost $100,000+ per week during festival season.

The Cannes Film Festival in May is the peak celebrity season, though it's extremely crowded and expensive. For a more relaxed experience with warm weather, June through September offers excellent beach weather and active nightlife. July and August see many celebrities on summer vacation. The shoulder seasons of April-May and September-October offer pleasant weather with fewer crowds.

During the Film Festival, the most exclusive parties happen at Hotel du Cap-Eden-Roc, on private yachts in the harbor, and at La Croisette beach clubs. Year-round hotspots include Baoli Beach, Le Gotha Club, and Les Marches restaurant. Many celebrities host private events at rented villas in the hills above Cannes or in nearby Saint-Tropez and Monaco.
