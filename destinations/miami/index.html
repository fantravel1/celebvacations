About Miami

Miami has transformed from a seasonal escape to a year-round celebrity playground, attracting stars with its unique blend of beach culture, international flair, and tax-friendly policies that have made it a primary residence for many.

The city's appeal extends beyond its famous beaches. Miami has become a genuine cultural hub, hosting Art Basel Miami Beach each December, Ultra Music Festival, Miami Swim Week, and Formula 1's Miami Grand Prix. This calendar of high-profile events ensures a constant rotation of celebrities, making Miami one of the most star-studded destinations in the Americas.

From South Beach's Art Deco glamour to the waterfront mansions of Star Island and Fisher Island, Miami offers celebrities both see-and-be-seen hotspots and ultra-private retreats.

Why Celebrities Love Miami

  • No state income tax—major draw for high earners

Frequently Asked Questions About Miami

Miami has become a celebrity residence hotspot, with permanent or frequent residents including David and Victoria Beckham, DJ Khaled, Shakira, Gloria Estefan, Marc Anthony, Lionel Messi, and many more. The city attracts visiting celebrities year-round, with peak activity during Art Basel (December), Miami Swim Week, Ultra Music Festival (March), and the F1 Miami Grand Prix (May).

Celebrity-favorite hotels include Faena Miami Beach (Art Basel headquarters), The Setai, Four Seasons Surf Club, Fontainebleau Miami Beach, and Edition Miami Beach. For longer stays, many celebrities own or rent homes on Star Island, Fisher Island (accessible only by ferry), the Venetian Islands, and in exclusive neighborhoods like Coconut Grove and Coral Gables.

The best time to visit Miami is November through April when the weather is warm but not oppressively humid. December is peak celebrity season due to Art Basel Miami Beach. Winter months (December-February) see the highest celebrity concentration as many escape colder climates. Summer is hot and humid with hurricane season (June-November), but offers lower hotel rates.
