About Monaco

Monaco is less a destination than a statement. This tiny principality—smaller than Central Park—concentrates more wealth, glamour, and celebrity presence per square meter than anywhere else on Earth.

Ruled by the Grimaldi family since 1297 and forever associated with Grace Kelly's fairy-tale marriage to Prince Rainier III, Monaco occupies a unique position in celebrity culture. It's both a tax haven attracting the ultra-wealthy as residents and a playground drawing visitors for its legendary events, casinos, and yacht-filled harbors.

The Monaco Grand Prix transforms the principality each May into the world's most glamorous sporting event, with celebrities watching from yachts and hotel terraces as Formula 1 cars scream through the streets. But Monaco's appeal extends year-round, from the Casino de Monte-Carlo to the exclusive beach clubs of Larvotto.

Why Celebrities Love Monaco

  • No income tax—attracts wealthy residents and extended stays
  • Monaco Grand Prix—the most glamorous race in motorsport
  • Casino de Monte-Carlo—legendary gaming and people-watching
  • Yacht culture—Port Hercules hosts the world's most impressive vessels
  • Proximity to Cannes, Nice, and the French Riviera
  • Royal family connections and aristocratic social scene

Frequently Asked Questions

Monaco is home to numerous celebrities who've established residency for tax benefits and lifestyle. Notable residents include F1 champions Lewis Hamilton and several other drivers, tennis star Novak Djokovic, Ringo Starr, Shirley Bassey, and many business moguls. The principality doesn't have income tax, making it attractive for high earners.

The Monaco Grand Prix weekend in late May offers the highest concentration of celebrities, with parties, yacht gatherings, and trackside appearances. The Monaco Yacht Show in September and various galas throughout the year also draw significant star power. Summer months see steady celebrity traffic at beach clubs and restaurants.

Monaco is among the world's most expensive destinations. Hotel rooms at top properties start around $500-1,000 per night normally but can exceed $10,000+ during Grand Prix weekend. Dinner at top restaurants runs $200-500 per person. Real estate averages over $60,000 per square meter—the highest in the world.
