About Courchevel

Courchevel is the undisputed king of luxury ski resorts—a glittering alpine enclave in the French Alps where the world's wealthiest and most famous gather each winter to ski pristine slopes and indulge in unparalleled opulence.

Part of the massive Les Trois Vallees ski area—the world's largest linked ski domain—Courchevel offers over 600 kilometers of interconnected pistes. But it's Courchevel 1850 (now officially branded simply as "Courchevel") that draws the celebrity crowds. This highest village features the resort's most prestigious hotels, designer boutiques rivaling Paris's finest, and an altiport that welcomes private jets landing directly on the mountainside.

The apres-ski scene in Courchevel is legendary. Champagne bars spill onto snow-covered terraces, Michelin-starred restaurants serve gastronomic masterpieces, and exclusive nightclubs host private parties for the global elite. The village streets are a parade of fur coats, custom ski wear, and the occasional supercar navigating snowy roads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Courchevel attracts an impressive roster of celebrities including David and Victoria Beckham (regular visitors with their children), Kim Kardashian, George Clooney, Prince William and Kate Middleton, Elton John, Leonardo DiCaprio, and numerous Russian oligarchs and Middle Eastern royalty. The resort is particularly popular during the Christmas and New Year period.

Luxury chalets in Courchevel range from EUR 50,000 to over EUR 500,000 per week during peak season. The most exclusive properties include private chefs, chauffeurs, ski instructors, and spa therapists. Hotels like Airelles and Cheval Blanc offer suites starting around EUR 3,000-5,000 per night, with top accommodations exceeding EUR 20,000 per night.

The ski season runs from December to April, with peak celebrity activity during Christmas week through mid-January. February school holidays also see high-profile visitors. For fewer crowds but excellent snow conditions, late January and March offer great value while still providing world-class skiing and access to all amenities.

Yes, Courchevel has its own altiport (LFLJ) with one of the world's most challenging runways—a steep 537-meter strip with an 18.5% gradient. Private jets and helicopters can land directly at 1850m altitude. However, most visitors fly into Geneva (2 hours), Lyon (2.5 hours), or Chambery (1.5 hours) and transfer by car or helicopter.
