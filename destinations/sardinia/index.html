About Sardinia

Sardinia's Costa Smeralda represents the pinnacle of Mediterranean luxury—a legendary stretch of coastline where billionaires' yachts anchor in crystalline bays and celebrities gather at exclusive beach clubs that define European glamour.

Created in the 1960s by the Aga Khan as a playground for the global elite, Costa Smeralda remains the ultimate Mediterranean destination for those who value both natural beauty and social prestige. Porto Cervo, its glamorous heart, transforms each summer into an open-air fashion show where superyachts worth hundreds of millions line the harbor.

The appeal extends beyond mere luxury. Sardinia offers dramatic granite cliffs, pristine beaches with impossibly turquoise water, and an Italian authenticity that more commercialized destinations lack. Celebrities come for the perfect balance: world-class service and infrastructure with genuine Mediterranean character, all protected by the natural exclusivity of island geography.

Why Celebrities Love Sardinia

  • Costa Smeralda's legendary beach clubs and nightlife scene
  • Porto Cervo marina—epicenter of global superyacht culture
  • Exclusive hotels designed for privacy and luxury
  • Stunning natural beauty with emerald waters and granite coastline
  • Easy yacht access to secluded coves and private beaches
  • Authentic Italian cuisine and world-class restaurants

Frequently Asked Questions

Sardinia attracts a diverse range of A-list celebrities including Leonardo DiCaprio, Beyonce and Jay-Z, George Clooney, Rihanna, and many tech billionaires like Jeff Bezos and Mark Zuckerberg. The Costa Smeralda is particularly popular during the summer months, transforming into an international celebrity gathering spot.

Top hotels like Hotel Cala di Volpe and Hotel Pitrizza range from $2,000-$8,000+ per night during peak summer season. Private villas can cost $15,000-$50,000 per week. Yacht charters in the area start around $50,000 per week for smaller vessels and can exceed $500,000 for superyachts.

The peak celebrity season runs from late June through August, with July being the busiest month. This is when Porto Cervo's social scene is at its most vibrant and superyachts fill the marina. September offers excellent weather with fewer crowds. The shoulder months of May-June and September-October provide beautiful conditions at lower prices.

Most celebrities arrive by private jet to Costa Smeralda Airport (Olbia), which sees heavy private aviation traffic during summer. Many also arrive by superyacht from other Mediterranean destinations like Monaco, St. Tropez, or the Amalfi Coast. Helicopter transfers between airport and hotels or yachts are common for those seeking maximum privacy.
