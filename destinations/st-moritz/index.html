About St. Moritz

St. Moritz isn't just a ski resort—it's the birthplace of winter tourism itself. Since 1864, when hotelier Johannes Badrutt famously bet his summer guests they'd enjoy the Alps in winter, this glittering Engadin valley town has defined alpine luxury.

Perched at 1,822 meters with an average of 322 days of sunshine annually, St. Moritz pioneered everything from alpine skiing to the bobsled, hosting two Winter Olympics (1928 and 1948). The resort's legendary status draws a who's who of celebrity visitors—from Hollywood A-listers and European royalty to tech billionaires and fashion icons.

What sets St. Moritz apart is its unique combination of world-class skiing, historic palace hotels, and an unmistakable air of old-money sophistication. The frozen lake becomes a stage for polo matches, horse racing (White Turf), and cricket tournaments, while Michelin-starred restaurants and exclusive members' clubs cater to the most discerning tastes.

  • Gateway to the glamorous Engadin valley and Italian border

Frequently Asked Questions

St. Moritz attracts a distinguished roster of celebrities including George Clooney, David and Victoria Beckham, Leonardo DiCaprio, Naomi Campbell, Elton John, and numerous European royals. The resort has long been favored by fashion industry figures, Formula 1 personalities like Bernie Ecclestone, and old-money European families who have vacationed here for generations.

Premium suites at St. Moritz's palace hotels range from CHF 2,000-10,000+ per night during peak season. Badrutt's Palace penthouses and Suvretta House's finest suites can exceed CHF 15,000 per night. Many celebrities opt for private chalets ranging from CHF 50,000-200,000+ per week during the winter season.

Peak celebrity season runs from December through early March, with the highest concentration during Christmas/New Year and late January through February for White Turf and Snow Polo. The resort enjoys over 300 days of sunshine annually, with summer (June-September) offering hiking, sailing, and cultural events at a more relaxed pace.

Most celebrities fly private to Samedan Airport (the highest airport in Europe, just 5km away) or Zurich, then helicopter to St. Moritz. The scenic Glacier Express train from Zurich (about 3.5 hours) is popular for those seeking a more relaxed arrival. Some drive from Milan (about 3 hours) for access to Italian shopping and dining.
