About Bora Bora

Bora Bora is the crown jewel of French Polynesia—a breathtaking volcanic island surrounded by a pristine turquoise lagoon that has captivated celebrities and honeymooners for decades. Its dramatic silhouette of Mount Otemanu rising from azure waters has made it the world's most recognizable tropical paradise.

The island pioneered the concept of overwater bungalows, and today these iconic accommodations define luxury travel. Perched above crystal-clear lagoons with glass floor panels for viewing marine life below, these bungalows offer a unique blend of intimacy and indulgence that nowhere else can replicate. For celebrities seeking the ultimate romantic escape, Bora Bora remains unmatched.

What sets Bora Bora apart is its perfect combination of natural drama and refined luxury. The volcanic peaks provide a stunning backdrop while the barrier reef creates a protected lagoon ideal for snorkeling, diving, and water sports. The intimate scale of the island—just 18 miles in circumference—ensures exclusivity while delivering world-class amenities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Bora Bora attracts numerous celebrities including Justin and Hailey Bieber (honeymoon), Jennifer Aniston, Nicole Kidman and Keith Urban, Eva Longoria, Reese Witherspoon, and many more. It's particularly beloved by newlywed celebrity couples and those celebrating romantic milestones.

Luxury overwater bungalows at top resorts range from $1,500-$8,000+ per night. Premium villas like the St. Regis Royal Estate or Four Seasons' largest accommodations can exceed $15,000-$30,000 per night. All-inclusive packages and private island experiences command even higher rates.

The dry season from May to October offers the best weather with lower humidity and minimal rainfall. Peak season runs from June through September when conditions are ideal. December through March is the wet season but still beautiful, with occasional tropical showers and warm temperatures.

Most celebrities fly to Tahiti's Faa'a International Airport (via Los Angeles or connecting flights), then take a 50-minute inter-island flight to Bora Bora. Resorts arrange boat transfers from the airport motu to the main resort. Some ultra-wealthy visitors arrive by private yacht or chartered seaplane.
