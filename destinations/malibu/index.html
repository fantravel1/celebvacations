About Malibu

Malibu represents the quintessential Southern California dream—27 miles of pristine coastline, dramatic mountain backdrops, and some of the most valuable real estate in the world.

Known as "The 'Bu" to locals, this beach city has been synonymous with celebrity culture since the Golden Age of Hollywood. From classic movie stars to modern tech billionaires, Malibu continues to attract those seeking the perfect blend of natural beauty, privacy, and proximity to Los Angeles.

The celebrity presence in Malibu is so significant that certain neighborhoods have earned nicknames—Carbon Beach is famously called "Billionaire's Beach" for its concentration of ultra-wealthy residents. Point Dume, Broad Beach, and the Malibu Colony are equally coveted addresses among the entertainment elite.

Why Celebrities Love Malibu

  • Unparalleled ocean views and beach access
  • Privacy and security in gated communities
  • Just 30-40 minutes from Hollywood and Beverly Hills
  • World-class dining at Nobu Malibu and other celebrity hotspots
  • Outdoor activities: surfing, hiking, horseback riding
  • Wellness culture with premium spas and retreats

Best Time to Visit Malibu

Malibu enjoys a Mediterranean climate with mild temperatures year-round, but celebrity activity and optimal beach weather vary by season.

Peak Season: June - August

Summer brings the warmest weather and highest celebrity presence. Expect to see A-listers at Nobu Malibu, on local beaches, and at exclusive parties. Book hotels well in advance.

Shoulder Season: May & September - October

Excellent weather with fewer crowds. September is particularly popular with celebrities seeking post-summer relaxation. Great time for wellness retreats.

Off-Season: November - April

Cooler temperatures and occasional rain, but still pleasant. Celebrities often stay at their Malibu properties year-round. Best rates on accommodations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Malibu

Malibu is home to numerous celebrities including Leonardo DiCaprio, Lady Gaga, Cher, Barbra Streisand, Cindy Crawford, and many others. The beach city has been a celebrity haven since the Golden Age of Hollywood. Celebrities are drawn to Malibu for its stunning beaches, privacy, and proximity to Los Angeles while offering an escape from city life.

The best time to visit Malibu is from late spring through early fall (May to October) when the weather is warmest and driest. Summer months (June-August) see the most celebrity activity at local restaurants and beaches, while September offers pleasant weather with fewer crowds. Even winter months are mild, with temperatures rarely dropping below 50°F.

Celebrities in Malibu often stay at luxury hotels like the Nobu Ryokan (a 16-room ultra-exclusive hotel), Malibu Beach Inn on Carbon Beach, or Calamigos Guest Ranch for wellness retreats. Many rent private beachfront estates or own homes along the exclusive Carbon Beach (known as 'Billionaire's Beach'), Point Dume, Broad Beach, and the Malibu Colony.

Nobu Malibu is the most famous celebrity dining spot, frequented by A-listers for its ocean views and Japanese cuisine. Other celebrity favorites include Geoffrey's Malibu (cliff-side dining), Malibu Farm (farm-to-table on the pier), Soho House Malibu (members-only), and The Sunset Restaurant. For casual spots, Paradise Cove Beach Cafe and Duke's Malibu are popular choices.
