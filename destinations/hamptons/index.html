The Hamptons

New York's Ultimate Summer Escape

Long Island, New York A-List Summer Capital
$50M+ Top Home Prices
2hrs From NYC
May-Sep Season

The Hamptons isn't just a destination—it's a social institution. Every summer, the South Fork of Long Island transforms into an extension of Manhattan's most exclusive circles, with billionaires, celebrities, and media moguls trading penthouses for oceanfront estates.

From intimate Bridgehampton to buzzy Montauk, each hamlet has its own personality and celebrity gravitational pull. Hedge funders fill Sagaponack, artists colonize Springs, and the fashion set stakes out East Hampton. Understanding this geography is essential to navigating the Hamptons celebrity scene.

Village-by-Village Guide

East Hampton

Celebrity Epicenter

The most glamorous and well-known Hamptons village. Pristine Main Street with designer boutiques, the legendary Nick & Toni's restaurant, and oceanfront estates that regularly sell for $20-50 million. Steven Spielberg, Jerry Seinfeld, and Martha Stewart all call East Hampton home.

East Hampton Essentials:

  • Main Beach: The iconic stretch of sand, lifeguard-protected
  • Nick & Toni's: The celebrity dinner spot since 1988
  • Guild Hall: Cultural center with A-list fundraisers
  • Maidstone Club: Ultra-exclusive private club (1891)

Montauk

The New Cool

Once a quiet fishing village, Montauk has become the Hamptons' coolest outpost. Gurney's Resort is the celebrity magnet—a beachfront property where rooms start at $800/night in summer. The Surf Lodge hosts legendary sunset concerts. Jay-Z, Beyoncé, and younger celebrities prefer Montauk's laid-back energy.

Bridgehampton

Horse Country

Home to the Hampton Classic Horse Show—a major social event where equestrian sport meets celebrity fashion. Beautiful farmland, excellent restaurants, and slightly more privacy than East Hampton. Bobby Flay and Ina Garten are Bridgehampton devotees.

Southampton

Old Money

The Hamptons' most traditional village. Grand estates along Gin Lane and Meadow Lane house families who've summered here for generations. Less celebrity-forward but equally exclusive. Bathing Corp. is the ultimate private beach club.

Sagaponack

Billionaire Belt

Consistently ranked among America's most expensive zip codes. Sprawling potato-field estates now sell for $30-100 million. Ultra-private, minimal commercial activity. Hedge fund titans and media moguls dominate here.

Amagansett

Artistic Soul

Retains a bohemian spirit despite rising prices. Paul McCartney, Alec Baldwin, and Drew Barrymore have properties here. The Lobster Roll (LUNCH) is a legendary roadside institution.

Sag Harbor

Literary Haven

A historic whaling port with charming Main Street. The American Hotel is a storied bar and inn. More village-y and walkable than other Hamptons communities. Colson Whitehead's novel "Sag Harbor" captures its cultural significance.

Where Celebrities Eat

Nick & Toni's

East Hampton

The undisputed celebrity dinner destination since 1988. Mediterranean-inspired cuisine with wood-fired oven. Getting a table in peak season is a status symbol. Spielberg, Seinfeld, and McCartney are regulars.

Dopo La Spiaggia

East Hampton

Italian restaurant owned by Marc Rowan. The hottest reservation in recent Hamptons summers. Creative Italian with impeccable ingredients.

The Surf Lodge

Montauk

Restaurant, hotel, and event space famous for sunset concerts. Acts from Puff Daddy to Mumford & Sons have performed. The see-and-be-seen summer venue.

Gurney's Montauk

Montauk

Beachfront resort with multiple restaurants. The outdoor Scarpetta Beach is perfect for celebrity-spotting over Italian cuisine and ocean views.

The Lobster Roll (LUNCH)

Amagansett

Iconic roadside spot since 1965. Everyone from Jerry Seinfeld to Jimmy Fallon stops here. Cash only, lines guaranteed, lobster rolls legendary.

Le Bilboquet

Sag Harbor

French bistro transplanted from the Upper East Side. Same celebrity clientele, same excellent steak frites, different zip code.

The Social Calendar

Memorial Day Weekend

Late May

The season opener. Summer rentals begin, restaurants open, and the first wave of celebrities arrives. House parties set the tone for summer.

Fourth of July

July

Peak Hamptons. Jerry Seinfeld's annual fireworks party is legendary. Every beach, restaurant, and club is at maximum capacity. Book months ahead.

Hampton Classic Horse Show

Late August

Week-long equestrian event that doubles as the summer's biggest social gathering. VIP tents, designer boutiques, and celebrity appearances throughout.

Labor Day Weekend

Early September

The grand finale. Farewell parties, last beach days, and the bittersweet end of another Hamptons summer. Prices drop dramatically after.

Where to Stay

Hotels are limited—most celebrities own or rent houses. But for visitors, these are the options.

Gurney's Montauk Resort

The Hamptons' most famous hotel. Direct beach access, spa, multiple restaurants, and the best sunset views on the South Fork. Summer rates from $800/night.

Topping Rose House

Bridgehampton's only luxury hotel. Beautifully restored 1842 house with farm-to-table restaurant by Jean-Georges. Only 22 rooms. From $600/night.

The Baker House 1650

East Hampton boutique inn with just 8 rooms. The most intimate luxury option, steps from Main Street. From $500/night.

Summer Rentals

The celebrity way: rent an entire estate for the season ($50K-$500K+ for summer). Monthly rentals from $15K for modest homes to $200K+ for oceanfront compounds.

Insider Tips

Getting There

  • Blade Helicopter: 40 min from Manhattan ($795+)
  • Jitney: Luxury bus, 2-3 hours ($40-55)
  • LIRR: Train to various stops ($20-30)
  • Drive: 2-4 hours depending on traffic

Etiquette

  • Don't photograph celebrities at restaurants
  • Casual luxury dress code everywhere
  • Reservations essential for top restaurants
  • Beach parking permits required (or walk)

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to visit the Hamptons?

The Hamptons season runs Memorial Day through Labor Day (late May to early September). July and August are peak season with the most activity and highest prices. June and September offer beautiful weather with fewer crowds.

Which celebrities live in the Hamptons?

Major celebrity homeowners include Jerry Seinfeld, Steven Spielberg, Beyoncé and Jay-Z, Paul McCartney, Robert Downey Jr., Jennifer Lopez, Martha Stewart, Alec Baldwin, and many more. Most own property in East Hampton, Bridgehampton, or Amagansett.

How expensive is a Hamptons summer rental?

Summer rentals (Memorial Day to Labor Day) range from $50,000 for a modest home to $500,000+ for an oceanfront estate. Monthly rentals range from $15,000 to $200,000+. Many homes require the full season commitment.

Where are celebrities most spotted in the Hamptons?

Nick & Toni's restaurant in East Hampton, The Surf Lodge in Montauk, Gurney's Resort, the Hampton Classic Horse Show, and various charity events are the top celebrity-spotting locations.
