Capri

The Eternal Isle of Glamour

Italy Legendary Jet-Set Destination
€800+ Luxury Hotel/Night
4 sq mi Island Size
May-Oct Season

Capri has been seducing the powerful and beautiful since Emperor Tiberius built twelve villas here two thousand years ago. This tiny island rising dramatically from the Bay of Naples concentrates glamour, natural beauty, and Italian style into four unforgettable square miles.

The appeal transcends eras: Roman emperors, Victorian poets, Hollywood legends, and today's celebrities all succumb to Capri's magic. The Blue Grotto's ethereal light, the Faraglioni sea stacks, the whitewashed piazzas—everything photographs beautifully, but the real enchantment is in the feeling of being somewhere truly special, elevated above ordinary life in every sense.

Capri vs. Anacapri

Capri Town

Glamour Center

The lower town with the famous Piazzetta—the "world's living room" where celebrities and socialites parade at sunset aperitivo hour. Designer boutiques (Prada, Gucci, Dolce & Gabbana), historic hotels, and the island's best restaurants cluster here.

Highlights:

  • La Piazzetta: The legendary people-watching square
  • Via Camerelle: Luxury shopping street
  • Gardens of Augustus: Stunning viewpoint
  • Marina Piccola: Beach clubs below

Anacapri

Quieter Elegance

The higher village offers a more relaxed atmosphere with fewer day-trippers. Villa San Michele, Monte Solaro chairlift, and Capri Palace (the island's most exclusive hotel) are here. More authentic Caprese life, less fashion parade.

Highlights:

  • Monte Solaro: Highest point, chairlift access
  • Villa San Michele: Axel Munthe's legendary villa
  • Capri Palace: Top luxury hotel
  • Blue Grotto: Access point nearby

Beach Clubs

Capri's rocky coastline means beach clubs are carved into cliffs—unique and spectacular.

La Fontelina

Most Iconic

The legendary beach club at the foot of the Faraglioni. Reach it by boat or 15-minute cliff walk. Sunbeds on platforms above crystal water, legendary pasta with zucchini, and the essence of Capri style. Reserve weeks ahead in summer.

Da Luigi ai Faraglioni

Classic Choice

Just around the rocks from La Fontelina, slightly more casual and easier to book. Same spectacular Faraglioni views, excellent seafood, and all-day Mediterranean lifestyle.

Il Riccio

Blue Grotto Area

Capri Palace's beach club with one Michelin star. The most refined dining on water, with seafood that rivals any restaurant on the island. Boat access or hotel transfer.

Lido del Faro

Anacapri

Peaceful club near the Punta Carena lighthouse. Locals' favorite, less touristy, stunning sunset position. Worth the effort to reach.

Essential Experiences

Blue Grotto

The legendary sea cave where light creates supernatural blue glow. Small boats enter through tiny opening—magical but brief (5 minutes). Go early morning to avoid crowds. Weather-dependent.

Faraglioni Boat Tour

Private boat circumnavigation of the island with swimming stops. Pass through the Faraglioni arch (tradition: kiss your partner), discover hidden coves, end with sunset champagne.

La Piazzetta Aperitivo

Sunset drinks at one of the four cafes ringing the tiny main square. People-watching is world-class. Arrive by 6pm for prime positioning; stay through dinner crowd transition.

Getting There

From Naples/Sorrento

  • Ferry: 50-80 minutes from Naples, 25 from Sorrento
  • Hydrofoil: Faster but rougher in bad weather
  • Private boat: Hotels can arrange transfers
  • Helicopter: Available but limited landing

On the Island

  • Funicular: Marina Grande to Capri town
  • Buses: Between towns and to beaches
  • Convertible taxis: Iconic open-top cars
  • Walking: The best way to explore

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I stay in Capri or visit as a day trip?

Stay overnight if budget allows. Day-trippers flood the island 10am-5pm; mornings and evenings reveal the real Capri. Sunset at La Piazzetta, dinner at a candlelit terrace, morning swims before crowds—these require an overnight stay.

How expensive is Capri?

Very expensive, especially in peak summer. Luxury hotels run €800-2,000/night. Dinner for two at top restaurants: €200-400. Beach club day: €100-200. Budget at least €500-1,000 per day for a luxury experience.

When is the best time to visit?

May-June and September-October offer ideal weather with fewer crowds. July-August is peak season—vibrant but crowded and expensive. Avoid Easter week and August holidays if seeking tranquility.
