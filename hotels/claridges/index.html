Claridge's

London's Legendary Art Deco Palace

5-Star Royal Warrant Mayfair, London
190 Rooms & Suites
£800+ Per Night
1856 Established

Claridge's isn't merely a hotel—it's a living institution of British luxury. Since 1856, this Art Deco masterpiece in the heart of Mayfair has been the London address of choice for royalty, heads of state, and the world's most celebrated entertainers.

Winston Churchill called it "an annexe to Buckingham Palace." During World War II, the Yugoslav government operated in exile from Suite 212. Today, the hotel maintains its position as London's ultimate luxury address, where afternoon tea is a ceremony and the doormen know your name before you arrive.

The Royal Connection

Claridge's relationship with royalty spans over 160 years.

Queen Victoria was an early patron, and every British monarch since has maintained the connection. The hotel holds a Royal Warrant and has hosted countless state visits. Most famously, Suite 212 was temporarily declared Yugoslav territory in 1945 so that Crown Prince Alexander could be born on native soil.

Royal Guests

Queen Elizabeth II, Prince Philip, Princess Diana, and numerous European royals have called Claridge's their London home.

State Visits

Foreign heads of state regularly stay during official visits. The hotel's security protocols rival those of embassies.

Royal Events

Wedding breakfasts, anniversary celebrations, and private royal dinners are regularly hosted in the hotel's legendary ballroom.

Fashion Week Central

During London Fashion Week, Claridge's becomes the industry's living room.

Designer Residences

Top designers book suites months in advance. Karl Lagerfeld, Donatella Versace, and Giorgio Armani have all made Claridge's their LFW home. Private fittings and press interviews happen in suites throughout fashion week.

The Foyer Scene

The art deco lobby becomes fashion's unofficial meeting point. Editors, models, buyers, and celebrities cross paths beneath the Dale Chihuly glass sculpture. The people-watching is unparalleled.

Dining & Experiences

Afternoon Tea at Claridge's

Iconic Experience

The Claridge's afternoon tea is a British institution. Served in The Foyer beneath the magnificent Dale Chihuly chandelier, it's a ritual that has remained largely unchanged for over a century. Finger sandwiches, freshly baked scones with Cornish clotted cream, and an exquisite selection of pastries accompany over 40 tea varieties.

Price: From £85 per person

Champagne Afternoon Tea: From £105 per person

Booking: Reserve 4-6 weeks ahead

Davies and Brook

Daniel Humm's London outpost brings his legendary New York cuisine across the Atlantic. The art deco dining room hosts a menu that celebrates British ingredients with refined technique. A destination restaurant in its own right.

Cuisine: Contemporary British-American

Dress Code: Smart Elegant

Claridge's Bar

Art Deco glamour meets exceptional mixology. Leather banquettes, mirrored walls, and a cocktail list that pays homage to the hotel's illustrious history. The Flapper, created in 1925, remains on the menu.

Signature: The Flapper (since 1925)

Live Music: Jazz pianist nightly

The Claridge's Experience

Claridge's Spa

Sanctuary of wellness featuring treatments by Bamford. The vitality pool, thermal suite, and expert therapists offer respite from London's pace.

Personal Butler Service

Assigned butlers for suite guests anticipate every need, from unpacking to securing impossible dinner reservations.

The Fumoir

Intimate cocktail den adjacent to the main bar. More private, more hushed, and perfect for discreet meetings.

Christmas at Claridge's

The annual Claridge's Christmas Tree is a London landmark.

The Designer Tree

Each year, a different fashion house or designer creates the lobby Christmas tree. Past designers include Karl Lagerfeld, Christian Louboutin, Dolce & Gabbana, and Burberry. The tree unveiling is a major event on the London social calendar.

Festive Season

The entire hotel transforms for the holidays. Special afternoon tea menus, festive cocktails, and a magical atmosphere make December at Claridge's an experience unto itself. Book months in advance.

Booking Tips

Fashion Week

Book 3+ months ahead for September and February fashion weeks. Rates increase significantly and the hotel fills with industry professionals.

Christmas Season

November and December book up fast. The Christmas Tree unveiling (usually late November) is especially popular. Book 4-6 months ahead.

Afternoon Tea

Reserve 4-6 weeks in advance, especially for weekends. Mention special occasions for potential upgrades.

Best Value

January through early February offers the best rates. Sunday nights typically have better availability than weekday stays.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Claridge's famous?

Claridge's is famous as London's most iconic luxury hotel, with over 165 years of history hosting royalty, celebrities, and world leaders. Its Art Deco architecture, legendary afternoon tea, and unparalleled service have made it a British institution. Winston Churchill famously called it "an annexe to Buckingham Palace."

What is the dress code at Claridge's?

Claridge's maintains a smart elegant dress code throughout the hotel. Gentlemen are required to wear a jacket in the restaurant and public areas after 6pm. Smart casual is acceptable during daytime, but sportswear, shorts, and trainers are not permitted.

How far in advance should I book Claridge's afternoon tea?

We recommend booking 4-6 weeks in advance, particularly for weekend sittings and special occasions. Holiday periods like Christmas require even earlier booking. Cancellation policies are strict, so book only when confirmed.

Do celebrities stay at Claridge's?

Yes, Claridge's has been the preferred London hotel for celebrities since the Golden Age of Hollywood. Stars from Cary Grant and Audrey Hepburn to modern A-listers like George Clooney, Beyoncé, and Taylor Swift have chosen Claridge's for its exceptional privacy and service.
