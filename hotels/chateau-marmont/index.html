Chateau Marmont

Hollywood's Legendary Hideaway Since 1929

5-Star Historic Landmark Sunset Boulevard, West Hollywood
63 Rooms & Suites
$600+ Per Night
1929 Established

Chateau Marmont isn't just a hotel—it's a monument to Hollywood mythology. Perched above Sunset Boulevard in a Gothic Revival castle, it has sheltered, inspired, and occasionally scandalized the entertainment industry for nearly a century.

The Chateau's appeal lies in its discretion. Staff are legendarily tight-lipped, and the property's layout—a main building surrounded by secluded bungalows and cottages—allows for maximum privacy. Harry Cohn's famous advice to starlets, "If you must get in trouble, do it at the Chateau Marmont," captures the hotel's enduring role as Hollywood's sanctuary for bad behavior and quiet recuperation alike.

Most Private

The Chateau's bungalows are the celebrity choice—standalone cottages hidden in the gardens with private entrances. Where deals are made and secrets kept.

Penthouses

Top-floor suites in the main building with sweeping views of LA. The penthouse where John Belushi died remains part of the hotel's mythology.

Cottages

Multi-room retreats for longer stays or those requiring more space. Many celebrities use these as LA pieds-à-terre between film projects.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Chateau Marmont so famous among celebrities?

Discretion, history, and tolerance. The staff never talk, the bungalows provide complete privacy, and the hotel's nearly century-long association with Hollywood makes it feel like industry sacred ground. It's where celebrities can truly let their guard down.

Can regular guests stay at Chateau Marmont?

The Chateau is now a members-only club and hotel. Membership is curated and exclusive, further cementing its status as an insider destination rather than a public hotel.

What makes the bungalows special?

The bungalows offer complete privacy—separate entrances, walled gardens, no housekeeping unless requested. Celebrities can come and go without passing through lobbies or encountering other guests. Many have kitchens for extended stays.
