About Four Seasons Bora Bora

Set on a private motu (small island) with unobstructed views of Mount Otemanu, Four Seasons Bora Bora has become synonymous with ultimate luxury escapes and is the gold standard for celebrity honeymoons and romantic getaways.

The resort features 107 overwater and beachfront bungalow suites, each offering direct lagoon access, glass floor panels for viewing marine life, and expansive outdoor living spaces. The property is accessible only by boat, ensuring exceptional privacy for high-profile guests.

What sets Four Seasons Bora Bora apart is the combination of iconic South Pacific beauty with impeccable service. The resort consistently ranks among the world's best, and its guest list reads like a who's who of Hollywood, music, and business royalty.

Frequently Asked Questions

Four Seasons Bora Bora has hosted numerous celebrities including Jennifer Aniston, Leonardo DiCaprio, Nicole Kidman and Keith Urban, Reese Witherspoon, Eva Longoria, and many others. It's particularly popular for celebrity honeymoons and milestone anniversaries.

Nightly rates at Four Seasons Bora Bora range from approximately $1,500 for beach bungalows to over $10,000 for the premier Otemanu Overwater Bungalow. Overwater bungalows with plunge pools typically range from $3,500-$6,000 per night. Peak season (June-October) commands higher rates.

The best time to visit is during the dry season from May to October, with August and September being peak months. The wet season (November-April) offers lower rates but more rainfall. Water temperatures remain warm year-round at 79-84°F (26-29°C).

Guests fly to Bora Bora Airport (BOB) on Air Tahiti from Tahiti-Faa'a International Airport (PPT). From the Bora Bora airport, the resort arranges a private boat transfer (approximately 20 minutes) to the private motu. The journey from Los Angeles takes about 8 hours to Tahiti plus connecting flights.
