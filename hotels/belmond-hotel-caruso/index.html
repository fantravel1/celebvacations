Belmond Hotel Caruso

An 11th-Century Palace Above the Clouds

5-Star Belmond Collection Ravello, Amalfi Coast
50 Rooms & Suites
€1,200+ Per Night
11th C. Original Palace

Perched 1,000 feet above the Amalfi Coast in the village of Ravello, Belmond Hotel Caruso occupies an 11th-century palace that feels suspended between sky and sea. Its infinity pool—arguably the most photographed hotel pool on Earth—appears to spill directly into the Mediterranean below.

The hotel's history reads like a novel. Originally built as a palace for noble families, it became a favorite of Virginia Woolf, D.H. Lawrence, and the Bloomsbury Group. Greta Garbo stayed here to escape Hollywood. Today, the same magnetic pull draws modern celebrities seeking the combination of ancient grandeur and natural spectacle that only Italy's Amalfi Coast delivers.

The Infinity Pool

The heated infinity pool is the hotel's crown jewel and one of the most recognizable images in luxury travel. Set at the edge of the garden terrace, the pool's water appears to merge seamlessly with the Gulf of Salerno below. At sunset, the water turns gold and pink, reflecting the sky in a display that has graced countless magazine covers and Instagram feeds.

The View

Panoramic 180-degree views encompassing the entire Gulf of Salerno. On clear days, you can see from Positano to Paestum. The terraced gardens frame the coastline below like a living painting.

Pool Service

Attentive poolside service with Champagne, cocktails, and light Mediterranean cuisine. Sun loungers are positioned for optimal views and privacy. Early morning swims are transcendent.

Ravello & Surroundings

Villa Rufolo

13th-century villa with gardens that inspired Wagner's Parsifal. The annual Ravello Festival hosts concerts against the coastline backdrop. Walking distance from the hotel.

Villa Cimbrone

The "Terrace of Infinity"—a belvedere with marble busts overlooking the coast. Greta Garbo walked these gardens. The most romantic viewpoint on the Amalfi Coast.

Path of the Gods

Legendary hiking trail connecting Agerola to Nocelle above Positano. Dramatic cliff-edge walking with panoramic coastal views. The Amalfi's most spectacular hike.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Belmond Hotel Caruso famous?

Belmond Hotel Caruso is famous for its heated infinity pool that appears to merge with the Gulf of Salerno 1,000 feet below—one of the most photographed hotel views in the world. The 11th-century palace setting, combined with its celebrity guest history (Greta Garbo, Virginia Woolf) and Ravello's dramatic position, make it one of Italy's most legendary hotels.

How do you get to Hotel Caruso from Naples?

The most comfortable option is a private transfer from Naples Airport (approximately 75 minutes). The drive along the Amalfi Coast is spectacular but winding. Helicopter transfers can also be arranged. The hotel concierge will organize transport upon booking.

Is Belmond Hotel Caruso worth the price?

For a once-in-a-lifetime Italian experience, yes. The combination of 11th-century architecture, the world-famous infinity pool, exceptional dining, and the most dramatic setting on the Amalfi Coast justifies the premium pricing. It's regularly ranked among the world's top hotels.
