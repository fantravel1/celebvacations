The Mark

Manhattan's Most Boldly Lavish Hotel

5-Star Celebrity Hotspot Upper East Side, New York
152 Rooms & Suites
$1,200+ Per Night
1927 Established

The Mark isn't just a hotel—it's a cultural institution. Positioned one block from Central Park and the Metropolitan Museum of Art, this Upper East Side landmark has become the definitive address for celebrities attending the Met Gala and A-listers seeking unparalleled Manhattan luxury.

Designed by Jacques Grange, The Mark blends Art Deco heritage with contemporary boldness. Its black-and-white striped awning is iconic, its service legendary, and its guest list reads like a who's who of global celebrity.

The Met Gala Headquarters

Every first Monday in May, The Mark transforms into fashion's command center.

The hotel's proximity to the Metropolitan Museum of Art—just a 3-minute walk—makes it the ultimate base for Met Gala preparation. Celebrities book entire floors months in advance, and the hotel's suites become temporary headquarters for the world's top glam squads.

Pre-Gala Prep

Suites are converted into hair and makeup studios. Multiple teams work simultaneously across rooms, with dress reveals happening in the Grand Penthouse.

The Red Carpet Exit

Celebrities descend The Mark's iconic staircase in full regalia, paparazzi lined up outside. It's fashion's most photographed hotel exit of the year.

After-Party Return

Post-gala, guests return for private celebrations. Room service operates around the clock, delivering everything from caviar to midnight pizza.

Booking Tips

Met Gala Week

Book 6+ months in advance for the first week of May. Rates increase significantly, and the Grand Penthouse is often booked a year ahead.

Best Value Periods

January-February and late August offer the best rates. Holiday season (November-December) sees increased demand.

Room Requests

Request 77th Street facing rooms for quieter stays. High floor suites offer better park views and more natural light.

VIP Treatment

Mention special occasions when booking. The Mark is known for extraordinary gestures—from flower arrangements to personalized amenities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do celebrities stay at The Mark?

The Mark offers unparalleled privacy, world-class service, and proximity to the Met for Gala week. Its discrete entrance, large suites for glam teams, and legendary concierge service make it the top choice for A-list guests. The hotel has perfected the art of celebrity hospitality over decades.

How much does The Mark Penthouse cost?

The Mark Penthouse—the largest hotel penthouse in the United States at 10,000 square feet—starts at $75,000 per night. During peak periods like Met Gala week, rates can exceed $100,000 per night.

Can I see celebrities at The Mark?

While The Mark is known for celebrity guests, the hotel prioritizes guest privacy. The restaurant and bar are your best chance for people-watching, particularly during fashion weeks and the Met Gala period.

Is The Mark worth the price?

For those seeking the ultimate Upper East Side luxury experience, The Mark delivers. The service, design, dining, and location justify the premium rates. It's particularly worthwhile for special occasions and those who appreciate true five-star hospitality.
