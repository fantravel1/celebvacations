The Little Nell

Aspen's Only Ski-In/Ski-Out Five-Star Hotel

5-Star Forbes Five-Star Relais & Châteaux Aspen Mountain, Colorado, USA
92 Rooms & Suites
$1,200+ Per Night
1989 Established
8,000 ft Elevation

The Little Nell isn't just Aspen's best hotel—it's the only address that matters for celebrities who take their skiing seriously. Positioned at the exact spot where Aspen Mountain meets the town, the hotel offers something no other five-star property can: step out your door, click into your bindings, and you're on the slopes.

Since opening in 1989, The Little Nell has defined luxury ski hospitality. The hotel combines the warmth of a mountain lodge with the sophistication of an urban boutique property. Belgian linens, gas fireplaces, marble bathrooms with heated floors, and mountain views from every room create a cocoon of comfort after days carving up Ajax (as locals call Aspen Mountain). But it's the service—a ski concierge who warms your boots overnight, a sommelier with 20,000 bottles at his disposal, a kitchen that earned Aspen its only Forbes Five-Star restaurant—that keeps A-listers returning season after season.

The Setting

The Little Nell occupies perhaps the most valuable piece of real estate in American skiing. The hotel sits at the base of the Silver Queen Gondola, directly at the bottom of Aspen Mountain's 3,267 vertical feet. To the left: the slopes. To the right: the restaurants, galleries, and boutiques of downtown Aspen. The location eliminates the friction that plagues most ski vacations—no shuttles, no parking hassles, no gear schleping across parking lots.

Ski Access

True ski-in/ski-out means exactly that: ski down Spar Gulch and glide to within steps of the hotel entrance. In the morning, the Silver Queen Gondola—Aspen Mountain's main lift—is a 30-second walk from the lobby. No other five-star hotel in Aspen offers this.

Town Access

Downtown Aspen begins at the hotel's front door. The Wheeler Opera House, high-end shopping on Galena Street, and Aspen's best restaurants are all within a 5-minute walk. During Food & Wine Classic weekend, the action literally surrounds the hotel.

Accommodations

Every room faces Aspen Mountain or offers courtyard views. All feature gas fireplaces, marble bathrooms, and The Little Nell's signature residential style.

The Christmas-New Year's Phenomenon

Between December 20 and January 3, The Little Nell becomes Hollywood's unofficial satellite office. Studio executives, A-list actors, and industry power players converge on Aspen, with The Little Nell serving as the social hub. Element 47 reservations become impossible to secure, and the lobby bar hosts more dealmaking than most agency offices. This tradition dates to the 1990s and shows no signs of slowing.

Dining

Element 47

Forbes Five-Star

Named for silver's atomic number (Aspen was built on silver mining), Element 47 is Aspen's only Forbes Five-Star restaurant. The kitchen focuses on contemporary American cuisine with Colorado influences—elk, trout, locally foraged ingredients. But the real star is the wine program: over 20,000 bottles and one of America's most awarded sommeliers.

Chair 9

The hotel's intimate bar, named after the old single chairlift that once served Aspen Mountain. Craft cocktails, après-ski bites, and a fireplace make it a cozy retreat. The bar's small size creates an exclusive atmosphere where guests mingle easily.

The Ski Concierge Experience

The Little Nell's ski concierge service sets the standard for luxury ski hotels. This isn't just equipment storage—it's a complete ski lifestyle management system that eliminates every friction point from the mountain experience.

Boot Warming

Leave your boots with the concierge each evening. They're dried, warmed overnight, and ready at your preferred departure time. No more cold, damp boots in the morning—a small luxury that makes a big difference.

Equipment Services

Complimentary overnight ski tuning. Demo skis available to try. Private fitting appointments with Aspen's best ski shops. The concierge tracks conditions and can recommend optimal equipment for the day.

Mountain Access

Private ski guides, first tracks programs, heli-skiing arrangements, and off-mountain adventures. The concierge knows the mountain intimately and can create customized ski days based on conditions and ability.

The Little Nell Story

Silver Mining Origins

The hotel is named for a character in Charles Dickens' "The Old Curiosity Shop"—a reference to Aspen's silver mining heritage when miners named their claims after literary and biblical figures. The Little Nell Mine was one of many that dotted Aspen Mountain in the 1880s. When the hotel opened in 1989, it reclaimed this name as a tribute to the town's history.

Aspen Skiing Company

Owned by the Aspen Skiing Company (which operates all four Aspen-area mountains), The Little Nell represents the company's commitment to hospitality excellence. This relationship provides guests with privileged access to the mountains—priority lift tickets, exclusive first tracks experiences, and insider knowledge that independent hotels can't match.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do celebrities stay at The Little Nell in Aspen?

The Little Nell offers unmatched convenience as Aspen's only ski-in/ski-out five-star hotel. Celebrities can ski directly to the hotel via the Silver Queen Gondola, avoiding shuttles and parking lots. The discreet service, exceptional dining at Element 47, and prime location at the base of Aspen Mountain make it the natural choice for high-profile guests who want to maximize slope time while maintaining luxury standards.

When is the best time to visit The Little Nell?

Christmas through New Year's is Aspen's busiest celebrity period, with The Little Nell at the center of the action. Mariah Carey's annual Christmas visits have made this period legendary. Presidents' Day weekend (mid-February) and spring break (March) also see significant A-list traffic. Summer brings a different crowd for hiking, biking, and the Aspen Music Festival.

What celebrities have stayed at The Little Nell?

The Little Nell has hosted virtually every celebrity who visits Aspen, including Mariah Carey (famous for annual Christmas visits), Kate Hudson and the Hudson-Hawn family, Heidi Klum, Antonio Banderas, Melanie Griffith, Kevin Costner, Jack Nicholson, and countless other A-listers. Aspen residents Goldie Hawn and Kurt Russell frequently dine at Element 47.

How much does The Little Nell cost per night?

Room rates at The Little Nell start around $1,200 per night in regular season and can exceed $5,000 during peak periods (Christmas, New Year's). The Grand Suite commands $8,000+ per night during holidays. Summer rates are significantly lower, starting around $700 per night.

Is The Little Nell family-friendly?

Yes, the hotel welcomes families and offers connecting rooms, multi-bedroom residences, and children's ski programs through the ski school. Many celebrity families choose The Little Nell specifically because the ski-in/ski-out access simplifies logistics with children. The Ajax Tavern is family-friendly for casual meals.
