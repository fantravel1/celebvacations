About The Ritz Paris

Since Cesar Ritz opened its doors in 1898, The Ritz Paris has been synonymous with ultimate luxury and has hosted the world's most illustrious guests. Located on the prestigious Place Vendome, it remains the definitive Parisian palace hotel.

The hotel's 142 rooms and suites blend 18th-century French elegance with modern amenities following a meticulous four-year restoration completed in 2016. Each room features antique furniture, marble bathrooms, and views of either Place Vendome or the hotel's beautiful gardens.

The Ritz Paris is inseparable from fashion history. Coco Chanel lived here for 34 years, and during Paris Fashion Week, the hotel transforms into an unofficial headquarters for the industry. Designers, models, editors, and celebrities make it their home base, continuing a tradition that stretches back over a century.

Living Legacy

Coco Chanel at The Ritz

Coco Chanel lived at The Ritz Paris for 34 years, from 1937 until her death in 1971, occupying a suite overlooking Rue Cambon—just steps from her famous atelier.

Her suite became her sanctuary, decorated with her signature style: beige, black, white, and gold, along with her beloved Coromandel screens. The current Coco Chanel Suite honors her legacy, featuring pieces inspired by her original apartment and items from the Chanel archives.

Chanel's residency at The Ritz cemented the hotel's connection to the fashion world, a relationship that continues today as designers, editors, and celebrities flock to the hotel during Paris Fashion Week. Her spirit permeates the property, from the Suite Coco Chanel to the boutiques that line Rue Cambon.

Room rates at The Ritz Paris start around $1,200 per night for a Superior Room. Suites range from approximately $3,000 to over $25,000 per night for the most exclusive accommodations like the Suite Imperiale. During Paris Fashion Week and peak seasons, rates can be significantly higher, and booking far in advance is essential.

Bar Hemingway is the legendary cocktail bar at The Ritz Paris, named after Ernest Hemingway who famously claimed to have "liberated" the bar during World War II. It's an intimate, wood-paneled space adorned with Hemingway memorabilia and photos. The bar is renowned for its expertly crafted cocktails and exclusive atmosphere, often requiring reservations.
