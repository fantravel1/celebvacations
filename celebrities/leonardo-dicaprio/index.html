Travel Profile

Leonardo DiCaprio is one of Hollywood's most eco-conscious travelers, known for balancing luxury vacations with environmental activism. His travel choices often reflect his commitment to sustainability and ocean conservation.

Born and raised in Los Angeles, DiCaprio maintains his primary residence in Malibu but is frequently spotted at some of the world's most exclusive beach destinations. He's particularly known for his love of yacht trips through the Mediterranean during summer months, often aboard rented superyachts with friends.

Beyond typical celebrity destinations, DiCaprio has made significant investments in eco-tourism, most notably purchasing Blackadore Caye in Belize to develop as a restorative eco-resort. His travel patterns show a preference for destinations with strong environmental credentials or opportunities for diving and ocean exploration.

Eco Investments

Blackadore Caye - DiCaprio's Private Island

In 2005, Leonardo DiCaprio purchased Blackadore Caye, a 104-acre island off the coast of Belize in the Caribbean, for a reported $1.75 million.

Rather than building a typical celebrity retreat, DiCaprio announced plans to develop the island as a completely sustainable, restorative eco-resort called "Blackadore Caye, A Restorative Island." The project aims to restore the island's degraded ecosystem, including mangrove restoration and manatee conservation.

The resort was designed to be entirely off-grid, powered by renewable energy, with structures built on stilts to minimize environmental impact. While the project has faced delays, it represents DiCaprio's commitment to combining luxury travel with environmental responsibility.
