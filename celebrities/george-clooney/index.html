Travel Profile

George Clooney essentially defined the celebrity vacation aesthetic of the 2000s. His purchase of Villa Oleandra on Lake Como in 2002 transformed the Italian lake into a celebrity pilgrimage site and established a template for sophisticated star travel.

Unlike celebrities who chase the latest hot destination, Clooney returns to the same beloved places year after year. Lake Como remains his summer anchor, the French Riviera draws him during Cannes, and since marrying human rights lawyer Amal Alamuddin in 2014, the couple splits time between their Los Angeles mansion, English country home in Berkshire, and the Italian villa.

Clooney's travel style emphasizes quality over quantity, discretion over documentation. He's rarely photographed on vacation by choice—sightings usually come from neighbors or fellow guests. With twins born in 2017, his travel has become more family-focused, though he still makes time for boys' trips with his famous circle of friends.

Legendary Property

Villa Oleandra, Lake Como

In 2002, George Clooney purchased Villa Oleandra in Laglio, a small village on Lake Como's western shore, for approximately $10 million. The 25-room 18th-century villa became the most famous celebrity home of the 21st century.

The property includes landscaped gardens descending to the lake, a swimming pool, outdoor theater, and garage for Clooney's vintage motorcycle collection. But more than its amenities, Villa Oleandra became legendary as Clooney's personal salon—a place where he hosted presidents, prime ministers, and Hollywood royalty.

Notable guests have included Barack and Michelle Obama, Bill and Hillary Clinton, Cindy Crawford and Rande Gerber, Matt Damon, Brad Pitt, and countless others. Clooney's presence transformed Lake Como from an Italian secret to a global celebrity destination, with tourism increasing dramatically.
